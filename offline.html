📖 Emergency Preparedness Guide

🌍
During an Earthquake
▼
  1. 1stDROP to the ground immediately — do not run.
  2. 2ndTake COVER under a sturdy table or cover your head with your arms.
  3. 3rdHOLD ON until the shaking completely stops.
  4. Stay away from windows, glass, and anything that could fall.
  5. Do NOT use elevators. Use stairs when exiting after shaking stops.
  6. After exiting, move to an open area away from buildings.
🌊
During a Flood
▼
  1. KeyMove immediately to higher ground — don't wait.
  2. Do NOT walk, swim, or drive through flood waters.
  3. 6 inches of moving water can knock you down.
  4. 1 foot of water can sweep a vehicle away.
  5. Stay off bridges over fast-moving water.
  6. Listen to authorities and do not return until cleared.
🐍
Snake Bite
▼
  1. DoStay calm and move away from the snake.
  2. Remove tight clothing and jewelry before swelling starts.
  3. Keep the bite area below heart level.
  4. Clean the wound gently with soap and water.
  5. Don'tDo NOT cut the wound or suck out the venom.
  6. Do NOT apply a tourniquet or ice.
  7. Seek medical help immediately — call 102.
🚗
Road Accident
▼
  1. 1stCheck the scene for safety before approaching.
  2. Call 102 (Ambulance) or 100 (Police) immediately.
  3. Do NOT move someone with a possible neck or back injury.
  4. Stop bleeding by applying firm pressure with a clean cloth.
  5. Keep the person warm and reassured.
  6. Do not give anything to eat or drink.
🔥
In Case of Fire
▼
  1. SmallUse extinguisher: Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep (PASS).
  2. LargeEvacuate immediately. Do not try to fight it.
  3. Feel doors before opening — if hot, use another exit.
  4. Stay low to avoid smoke inhalation while escaping.
  5. Once out, stay out — never re-enter a burning building.
  6. Call Fire Brigade: 101 from a safe location.
🩺
Heart Attack Signs
▼
  1. SignsChest pain, shortness of breath, sweating, arm/jaw pain.
  2. Call 102 immediately — do not wait.
  3. Have the person sit or lie down in a comfortable position.
  4. Loosen tight clothing around neck and chest.
  5. If trained, be ready to perform CPR if they become unresponsive.
  6. Stay calm and keep the person calm until help arrives.

🛡️ About Emergency Seva

🇳🇵
Built for Nepal
Emergency Seva provides instant access to emergency contacts across all 7 provinces and 77 districts of Nepal — in both Nepali and English.
📶
Works Offline
All contact data is embedded in the app. Once loaded, it works without an internet connection — critical when networks are down during emergencies.
📞
One-Tap Calling
Every contact has a direct call button. No copying numbers, no searching. In an emergency, every second counts.
📍
Location Aware
Set your province and district once and instantly see the relevant local emergency contacts for your area.
⭐
Favorites & Custom
Save your most-used contacts to Favorites. Add your personal emergency contacts like family doctors, neighbors, or local helpers.
📱
Emergency SMS
Send your GPS location to an emergency contact with one tap — helpful when you cannot speak or describe your location.
⚠️
Important Disclaimer: Emergency Seva is NOT an official government application. It is a community tool built to help people find emergency contacts faster. In any life-threatening situation, always call 100 (Police), 102 (Ambulance), or 101 (Fire) first. Contact numbers may change — please verify critical numbers in advance.
